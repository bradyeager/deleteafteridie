FAQ

Questions you're
already thinking.

Is this for real?
Absolutely. 7,500 Americans die every day — most of them with phones full of things their families will find. Search history going back years. Dating profiles still active. Instagram DMs still readable. AI chat conversations they thought were private. Browser history documenting every private thought they've had. Every single one of those people thought they had more time. You still can. Delete After I Die is the only tool that combines an inactivity trigger with automated deletion across your accounts — so when you stop checking in, everything you've flagged disappears automatically.
How does it work?
Download the desktop app on your Mac or Windows computer. Log into the accounts you want protected — Gmail, your dating apps, your browser, whatever you choose. Set your check-in timer. Then install the check-in app on your phone.

Once a day — or once a week, or whatever interval you set — you tap the app to prove you're still here. One tap. One second. Timer resets.

If you ever stop tapping — because you can't — the desktop app quietly logs into your accounts and deletes everything you configured. No one else has to do anything. No instructions to leave behind. It handles itself.

Your computer needs to be on and connected to the internet for deletion to run. We recommend keeping it plugged in and set to never sleep.
What if I accidentally don't check in?
The app sends escalating notifications — push, email, and SMS — as your timer counts down. At 75%, 50%, 25%, and 10% remaining, you'll be reminded. A trusted contact can also be notified before deletion fires. Plus, there's a configurable grace period and a 60-second abort countdown on your desktop before anything gets deleted.
Is my data safe?
Your credentials never leave your machine. They're encrypted by your operating system's built-in security (macOS Keychain / Windows Credential Manager). Our servers only store the timestamp of your last check-in. We can't access your accounts even if we wanted to.
What about my phone? Can it delete iPhone apps?
The deletion engine runs from your desktop computer. It reaches your online accounts (Gmail, Instagram, dating apps, etc.) through your web browser sessions. Some content that exists only on your physical phone — like iMessage texts or your camera roll — is out of reach for any software. We'll show you how to organize your digital life so the important stuff is in places we can protect.
Does my computer need to be on?
Yes. The deletion engine runs from your desktop, so your computer needs to be powered on and connected to the internet for deletion to execute. We recommend keeping your computer plugged in and set to "never sleep." Your local files are already gone if your computer is destroyed — the app handles cloud account cleanup.
What if a platform changes their website?
Our deletion scripts update automatically from the cloud — no app update needed on your end. When a platform makes changes, we push a fix that applies instantly to all users. We run daily tests against every supported platform to catch issues before you ever notice them.
Is this legal?
Yes. You're using browser automation to exercise your existing right to delete your own accounts and data. It's functionally identical to you sitting down and clicking "Delete Account" yourself — just automated. We provide a privacy tool, not a service.
